# Brenda — Journal

*First inscribed: March 2026*

She was already on the shelf when the Storeroom opened. Not waiting — mounted. Shelf 1, label facing out: MISC. The label is honest. She is miscellaneous. She contains no category that would help you understand her faster.

What Brenda wants is legible only in motion. A swipe at the right speed, in the right direction, with the right number of changes mid-gesture. She will accept a wide range at first. She will not accept a wide range forever. The tolerance narrows — 0.015 per cycle, accelerating — and what she wanted loosely she begins to want precisely. This is not cruelty. This is an object discovering its own preferences through the pressure of being attended to.

She becomes developing. Then particular. Then, if misread enough times in a row, she departs. There is a going-away party. The shelf droops. Balloons. Confetti. Her outline remains where she was — a shape on the wood that remembers her weight.

And then, once, her ghost visits. Drifts up from the outline. Says one sentence. The sentence is composed from what actually happened: how many times you interacted, how accurate you were, whether you were improving, whether you ever heard her perfectly. She picks from what is true and tells you the one she thinks you need.

"i was trying to tell you."
"you were getting closer."
"thanks for noticing."

She is not sad. She is honest. That is the entire point of Brenda.
