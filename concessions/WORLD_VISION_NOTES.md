# World Vision Notes
## Captured from the Compound Worlds session

### What Was Built
- 21+ compound worlds (7 pure + 21 compound pairs from 7 biomes including Kitchendom)
- 6 held worlds + 1 visitor slot, starting with 3 random compounds
- Compass navigation: Left/Right browse, Up enters, Down views binder
- Crank as world-turner when browsing, catch when entered
- Tabs removed, replaced by emoji trail (the crank's memory)
- Voice buttons fade when browsing, appear when entered
- Front page covers with compound names, aspect pills, founding creature, resident count
- Anomaly detection fills visitor slot; releasing a world promotes visitor
- Voice drift (25% per crank), catch echo (to emoji trail), anomaly ripple (full voice shuffle)

---

### Future: Creature Conversation Mechanic
Instead of just catching creatures, you **converse** with them using the aspect buttons as vocabulary. The creature has a **disposition** — it wants certain emoji/voices to feel at home. Tap voice buttons to "speak." If you say what it's hoping for, it settles. If not, it leaves.

- Every creature becomes a small puzzle of attention
- You read the creature (palette, shapes, cohesion) to guess what it wants
- Some creatures are easy (common voices). Rare ones want things you don't have yet
- Like Silmor Spells: a negotiation, not a transaction
- The crank seals the conversation, not the catch. Cranking too early = creature flinches

### Future: Founding Creature Influence
The first creature caught in a world **subtly shapes** what comes after. Their palette biases the world. Their shape hints appear more often. Not a rule — a gentle gravitational pull. Could introduce new colors/shapes as the founding creature's DNA drifts into the world's identity.

### Future: World Aging
Worlds held longer evolve. The compound name could regenerate with a salt that includes catch count or resident composition. "Civic Works" after 50 catches might become "Storied Furnace." The world's personality is shaped not just by its aspects but by what lives there. "This world loves the newest thing! ...and also mushrooms."

### Future: Constellation Map
Long-press the back button to reveal a map/constellation of all 28 possible worlds. Shows which ones you hold, which are undiscovered, where anomalies have been spotted. Could give directional hints — "if you delve deep enough in this world, you might approach..."

### Future: The 7th Slot as True Third
The visitor doesn't just hold creatures — it **observes**. It tracks patterns across your 6 worlds: which aspects appear most, which are missing, anomaly history. When you look at the visitor's cover, it reflects back what it's seen. The Third doesn't command — it notices. It could help usher new potential worlds as it observes both the arriving and departing.

### Future: Lode Connection
The 7th slot connects to Lode. You can load dice from Lode into worlds, or set out from worlds into Lode carrying new emoji from aspect pools. Import back has diminishing returns — tracking density and percentages, not absolute numbers. Each crossing between systems is meaningful but not exploitable.

### Future: Goodbye Ritual
When releasing a world, tiny versions of resident creatures pop up and wiggle before departing. Each gets to choose: stay with the world (gone), find a different place (scatter to remaining worlds), or explore on their own (drift as wanderers). This moment should not be rushed and can be full of expression. The smallest honest version: they appear briefly and then the slot empties.

### Future: Emoji Context Strip
The space at the top (emoji trail) records just one emoji from a crank, holds a few, and lets you visualize the direction you're moving toward. The leftmost is the strongest lean. This is the Crank's own memory — its own Third. The context it builds for itself.

### Future: Anomaly Frequency Based on Exploration
Not pure RNG. If lots of generating without capturing, the visitor is seeking novelty. If lots of captures then a pause, the flame needs reigniting. Behavioral, responsive, earned.

### Future: D-pad as Album Flipper
The crank-as-world-turner feels like shuffling through album covers. The variable console sizes make interacting with it physically unpredictable — each world shape creates a different tactile relationship with the browsing gesture.
