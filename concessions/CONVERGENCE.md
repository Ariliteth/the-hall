# Convergence

A single experience built from systems prototyped across the Hall's scores.

---

## The Player

You are the advancing force. You move forward through territory like Lode's die — stomping, accumulating, growing. The stomp has weight: formations scatter or crash backward as you push through. Your empire is not measured in grain or coin but in things you have chosen to collect. Your identity is chromatic, emergent, yours.

## The Generals

Two generals flank you — one left, one right. They walk with you. They have personality, comfort, grudges, history (Bao's system). They speak: short gab lines about what they see, how they feel about the next obstacle, whether they think they can handle it.

When a dispatch arrives (via S.Mail arrangement — an emoji strip, no translation), you must decide what to do. Each flanking general offers their read: confidence, a plan, a warning. After a beat, a third voice (the advisor, SILMOR's pattern) may offer a suggested plan with a high chance of success and drawn from your other generals and resources.

If you send a general, they leave your flank. You feel the absence — one side is empty. Another general you have, if any, takes their place. They appear as a minimized chat bubble on your HUD. You hear from them when they report:

- Sometimes asking for something
- Sometimes offering something
- Sometimes just checking in
- Sometimes silence — and you must send your own messenger
- Sometimes the line goes quiet for too long

When they return, they don't snap back to your side. They enter the queue on one of your flanks and rejoin you after some moves. The reunion is earned by proximity, not instant.

## Accumulation

Lode's system: as you stomp forward, you accumulate. But the resources are not preset categories. They are things you have chosen to gain — your emblem, your identity, your collected weight. The empire you build reflects decisions, not optimization.

## The Map

Context Window's tile memory and waypoint paths provide the overarching mission structure. You navigate territory like walking through Sunset Ridge Mall — a place that is alive around you, with memory of where you've been and markers for where you're going. Waypoints are your strategic objectives. The path between them is the march.
